Welcome to Kollam

Kollam also known by its former name Quilon is an ancient seaport and city on the Malabar Coast of India It is 66 km (41 mi) north of the state capital Thiruvananthapuram. The city is on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake and the Kallada river. Kollam has a strong commercial reputation since ancient times. The Arabs, Phoenicians, Chinese, Ethiopians, Syrians, Jews, Chaldeans and Romans have all engaged in trade at the port of Kollam for millennia. Kollam is also home to one of the seven churches that were established by St Thomas as well as one of the 10 oldest mosques believed to be found by Malik Deenar in Kerala.

Palaruvi Water Falls

Palaruvi waterfalls is the 32nd highest waterfalls in India. Located in the sleepy village of Aryankavu in the district of Kollam

Jatayupara

The spot is famous for the giant rock sculpture of Jatayu, the mythical bird of the epic Ramayana.

Tangasseri Lighthouse

Tangasseri Lighthouse or Thangassery Lighthouse is situated at Tangasseri in Kollam city of the Indian state of Kerala
